Info & How to Play

This game tests your ability to distinguish between Major 7 and Dominant 7 chords! Both of these chords start with a standard Major triad, but they differ in the 4th note (the 7th interval) added on top.

Once you are in the game, press Begin Game to start. Listen carefully to the chord and decide which quality you hear.

Use the generated answer buttons (or your keyboard's Left/Right Arrow keys) to answer. You can press Replay Chord or 'R' to listen again. Use Next or Spacebar to proceed.

More information 🧐

A 7th chord is constructed by taking a standard triad and stacking an extra third on top. Both of the chords in this game are built on a Major Triad, but feature a different 7th note:

Major 7: Root + 4 semitones + 3 semitones + 4 semitones
Dominant 7: Root + 4 semitones + 3 semitones + 3 semitones

Listen for the emotional qualities of these chords. Major 7ths often sound dreamy, jazzy, or resolved. Dominant 7ths feature a flatter, "minor" 7th interval which makes them sound bluesy, tense, and unresolved (like they want to lead into another chord).
